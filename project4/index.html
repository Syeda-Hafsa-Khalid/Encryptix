Tribute to Abdul Sattar Edhi - A Real Life 'Super-Hero'

Abdul Sattar Edhi, often referred to as 'Baba (Father) Edhi', the 'Richest Poor Man', and the 'Angel of Mercy', was a Pakistani humanitarian, philanthropist, and saint. He founded the Edhi Foundation, which operates the world's largest volunteer ambulance network. The foundation also provides free educational services, orphanages, homeless shelters, animal shelters, rehabilitation centers, hospitals, free kitchens (Langar Khana), and morgue services across Pakistan.

Admirable Qualities

Abdul Sattar Edhi is renowned for his profound compassion and humanity, dedicating his life to aiding those in need, regardless of their background, and often foregoing personal comforts to serve others. His relentless work ethic and unwavering commitment to improving millions of lives highlight his dedication and perseverance. As a visionary leader, he founded the Edhi Foundation, offering innovative social services and creating an inclusive environment where people from all walks of life could contribute and benefit. Known for his integrity and humility, Edhi managed funds transparently and lived a modest lifestyle, earning widespread respect. Despite facing numerous challenges, his courage and resilience enabled him to advocate for justice and stand up for the oppressed. Through his efforts, he empowered communities by providing resources and support, and promoted education to ensure the less fortunate had access to learning opportunities.

Awards And Honors

These awards and honors reflect Abdul Sattar Edhi's immense impact and dedication to humanitarian causes, cementing his legacy as one of the greatest philanthropists and social workers of our time.

National Awards

In 1989 he received Nishan-e-Imtiaz One of the highest civilian awards in Pakistan, recognizing his outstanding public service.

In 2010 he received Hilal-e-Imtiaz Another prestigious award given by the Government of Pakistan for his exceptional contributions to the country.

International Awards

Ramon Magsaysay Award for Public Service (1986): Often considered the Asian equivalent of the Nobel Prize, awarded for his selfless public service.

Lenin Peace Prize (1988): Awarded by the Soviet Union for his humanitarian efforts.
